II

Acupuncture

Most people describe a mild aching, tingling or heavy sensation rather than sharp pain. We use very fine needles — much thinner than injection needles. There may be a brief prick on insertion; during retention most patients find it comfortable, and many rest or fall asleep. Let Dr. Ng know if you are nervous — he can adjust his technique.

Yes. We use internationally standard single-use sterile needles, disposed of immediately after each patient. Hygiene and safety are fundamental to our practice.

Avoid strenuous exercise for about 2 hours; drink warm water; avoid alcohol on the day; keep needle sites dry for about 4 hours. Mild bruising is normal and usually fades within a few days.

Acupuncture during pregnancy requires special care — certain points are contraindicated. Please inform us when booking if you are pregnant; Dr. Ng will assess suitability and adjust the treatment plan accordingly.

III

Herbal Medicine

Under the supervision of a qualified practitioner, herbal medicine is generally well tolerated. Dr. Ng prescribes according to your constitution and avoids ingredients that may cause discomfort. Some people experience brief digestive adjustment when starting — contact us promptly if you have any concerns so your formula can be adjusted.

Some herbs may interact with Western drugs and require careful management. Tell Dr. Ng about all medications and supplements at your first visit. He will advise on appropriate timing — typically at least one hour apart from Western medication.

Traditional decoctions vary in taste — some are bitter. We also offer concentrated granule formulas for easier preparation. Let Dr. Ng know your preference and he will accommodate where possible.
