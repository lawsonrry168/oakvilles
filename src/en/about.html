Rooted in Tradition

Theory as foundation, experience as proof

With twenty-five years of TCM practice in Hong Kong and a Google Maps rating of 5.0 / 5 (3 verified reviews), Dr. Ng has spent the past decade focusing on stubborn eczema, acne and other skin conditions, as well as gynaecological and reproductive health. His core approach — treating internal and external factors together — combines pattern-based diagnosis with oral and topical herbal medicine, acupuncture and personalised lifestyle guidance to support your path back to wellbeing.

For skin conditions, Dr. Ng addresses underlying patterns such as lung–spleen damp-heat and liver stagnation with blood stasis, combining oral and topical herbs with acupuncture. Many chronic skin cases show gradual improvement with sustained care; individual progress depends on constitution and duration of the condition.

In gynaecological care, he supports stress-related subfertility through nourishing yin and blood with herbal formulas, combined with Ren and Du meridian acupuncture to support ovarian function and uterine health. A course of three to nine months is typically recommended, with menstrual regularity and overall wellbeing monitored over time.

Our Approach

Core Treatment Philosophy

I

Internal & External Together

TCM views skin problems as arising from internal deficiency meeting external pathogenic factors — spleen and stomach imbalance leading to dampness accumulation, which then triggers symptoms when combined with external triggers. Treatment cannot rely on topical products alone; oral herbs strengthen the spleen and stomach, topical washes or ointments clear heat and toxins, and acupuncture helps regulate immunity — working from the root to support allergic constitutions and reduce recurrence.

II

Pattern-Based Treatment

Through the four diagnostic methods (inspection, listening and smelling, inquiry, palpation), Dr. Ng identifies the primary pattern and treats accordingly. Acne in different facial zones reflects different organ imbalances — forehead lesions often relate to heart fire, while chin acne commonly links to hormonal imbalance. Personalised prescriptions may help reduce recurrent flare-ups.

III

Root & Branch Together

In the early phase, treatment focuses on clearing heat and toxins and resolving dampness to manage symptoms; once stable, formulas that soothe the liver, regulate qi and nourish blood and spleen help consolidate progress and support long-term wellbeing.
